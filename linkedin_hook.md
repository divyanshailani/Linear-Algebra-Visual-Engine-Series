I spent the last month building a 3D Linear Algebra visualization engine from scratch. 

No textbooks. No black-box math libraries to do the heavy lifting of the visuals. Just Python, NumPy, and Blender's `bpy` API.

My philosophy has always been: "I like building from first principles: prove the idea in code, then make it visual enough to teach." 

So I challenged myself to build a 5-part architecture to geometrically prove the hardest concepts in Linear Algebra:
1️⃣ **Basis Vectors & Space Lattice:** Proving that a matrix is just a spatial transformation of space.
2️⃣ **Change of Basis:** Tracking an "anchor vector" across two overlapping coordinate systems.
3️⃣ **Cramer's Rule in 3D:** Proving that solving a linear system is literally just finding the ratio of 3D parallelepiped volumes.
4️⃣ **Eigenvectors:** Visually isolating the exact axes of space that survive a complex transformation, stretching by their eigenvalue.

I've packaged all 5 physics engines into a Master Showcase Repository. If you're interested in graphics programming, technical animation, or just want to see math look cinematic, check out the source code and the renders here. 👇
